Biography

I am a double-degree PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of Eastern Finland and Linnaeus University. In my PhD research, I am supervised by Prof. Pasi Fränti and Prof. Mikko Laitinen. My research focuses on the clustering and modeling of large-scale social networks, and my primary area of study involves extensive datasets obtained from Twitter (recently renamed X). Additionally, my research extends to gathering and analyzing data from various other social media platforms, including Facebook, YouTube, and Discord, as part of my academic studies. I also hold a bachelor's degree in Software Engineering and a master's degree in Artificial Intelligence and Robotics.

I moved to Finland (Joensuu) in 2018. Joensuu is located in the heart of Finland's eastern region of North Karelia. It is a place where coffee consumption is as much a survival strategy as it is a social pastime and where the locals might tell you, half-jokingly, that the city has two seasons: winter and July. I came here in 2018 first as a project researcher, and later, in 2019, I started my doctoral studies.

Nordic Tweet Stream (NTS)

Link: https://nordictweetstream.fi
Description: NTS is a web application tool that we developed as part of a project funded by the Research Council of Finland and its Research Infrastructure program. It allows users to search, subset, visualize, and download user-generated social media data from the Nordic region. The NTS dataset contains nearly 74 million messages from over 888 thousand user accounts from January 2013 to May 2023. We hosted the tool on CSC cPouta, which is based on OpenStack. The backend and database primarily utilize Django, Elasticsearch, and PostgreSQL.

Nordic Twitter Network 2022 (NTN-2022)

Link: https://github.com/NTN_2022
Description: The NTN-2022 dataset is a network dataset focused on Twitter users within the Nordic countries between November 2016 and November 2022. This dataset is intended for academic research and analysis focused on the empirical structure of social relations within the Nordic Twittersphere. Users are encouraged to cite our paper in any resulting work.
